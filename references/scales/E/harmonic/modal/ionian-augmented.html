Scale: Harmonic in E (Mode: Ionian augmented)

Overview

The harmonic scale in E, in the mode of Ionian augmented, is a unique and colorful scale that is not commonly used in mainstream music but can add a distinctive sound to compositions. This scale is derived from the E harmonic minor scale but with a raised 4th degree, creating an augmented 4th interval between the 3rd and 4th notes. The resulting sound is exotic and mysterious, making it a popular choice for creating tension and drama in music.

Notes and Intervals

The E harmonic scale in Ionian augmented mode consists of the following notes:

- E (Root)

- F# (Major 2nd)

- G# (Major 3rd)

- A# (Augmented 4th)

- B (Perfect 5th)

- C (Major 6th)

- D# (Major 7th)

The intervals in this scale are as follows:

- Root to Major 2nd: Whole tone

- Major 2nd to Major 3rd: Whole tone

- Major 3rd to Augmented 4th: Half tone

- Augmented 4th to Perfect 5th: Whole tone

- Perfect 5th to Major 6th: Whole tone

- Major 6th to Major 7th: Whole tone

- Major 7th back to Root: Half tone

Musical Genre and Flavor

The harmonic scale in E Ionian augmented mode is often used in genres that require a sense of tension and exoticism. It can be found in various forms of world music, fusion jazz, and experimental compositions. The augmented 4th interval introduces a dissonant and unstable quality to the scale, creating a feeling of unease or suspense in the music. This scale is not typically used in conventional pop or rock music but can add a unique flavor to compositions that aim to evoke mystery or intensity.
